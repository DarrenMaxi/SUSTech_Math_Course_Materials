Step-1

By the properties of determinants, we know that multiplying a row with 3 of a matrix lead to the determinant is increased by 3 times.

So, det B = 3 det A

Also, by the properties of determinants we have that subtracting a row from another row does not change the determinant.

So, det B = det C.

Therefore, the determinant of the resultant matrix C is three times the determinant of the given matrix A.
